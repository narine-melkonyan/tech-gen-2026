SDLC

What is the Software Development Life Cycle?

SDLC is a structured process that defines how software is planned, created, tested, and delivered. It gives the entire team a shared framework so everyone knows what happens, in what order, and who is responsible.

STLC

What is the Software Testing Life Cycle?

STLC is a sequence of specific activities performed during testing to ensure quality goals are met. It runs parallel to SDLC — not after it.

Key insight:

QA does not wait for development to finish. Testing activity starts from the Requirements phase.

QA ROLE

The QA Engineer's Role

QA is not just a tester who clicks buttons at the end. A QA engineer is involved from day one and is responsible for quality across the entire lifecycle.

Common misconception: "QA tests at the end."
Reality: QA is involved from requirements to post-deployment.
